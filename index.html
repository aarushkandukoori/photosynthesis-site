What is Photosynthesis?

Photosynthesis is the biochemical process by which organisms use light energy to synthesize organic compounds from carbon dioxide and water.

☀️

Light Energy

Sunlight is captured by chlorophyll pigments inside chloroplasts, primarily in plant leaves.

💧

Water

Absorbed through roots, water is split during the light-dependent reactions, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.

🌬️

Carbon Dioxide

CO₂ enters leaves through tiny pores called stomata and is fixed into glucose during the Calvin cycle.

🍃

Glucose

The sugar produced stores energy and serves as building blocks for cellulose, starch, and other biomolecules.

The Chemical Equation

Photosynthesis can be summarized in one elegant reaction:

6 CO2 + 6 H2O + light energy → C6H12O6 + 6 O2

Six molecules of carbon dioxide and water, powered by sunlight, produce one glucose molecule and six oxygen molecules.

~50% of Earth's oxygen comes from ocean phytoplankton
100+ billion tons of CO₂ fixed by photosynthesis each year
3.5 billion years — how long photosynthesis has shaped our atmosphere

Two Stages of Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis occurs in two interconnected stages inside the chloroplast.

01

Light-Dependent Reactions

Thylakoid membranes
  • Chlorophyll absorbs photons of light
  • Water molecules are split (photolysis)
  • ATP and NADPH are produced
  • Oxygen is released into the atmosphere
Light → ATP + NADPH → O₂
02

Calvin Cycle

Stroma
  • CO₂ is fixed by the enzyme RuBisCO
  • ATP and NADPH from Stage 1 power the cycle
  • 3-carbon molecules (G3P) are formed
  • G3P is assembled into glucose
CO₂ → G3P → Glucose

Factors That Affect Photosynthesis

The rate of photosynthesis depends on environmental conditions. Drag the sliders to see how each factor influences the process.

Photosynthesis Rate
72%

Optimal conditions — photosynthesis is running efficiently.

Light

Too little light limits energy capture. Beyond a saturation point, more light doesn't increase the rate.

CO₂

Carbon dioxide is often the limiting factor in enclosed environments. Higher CO₂ can boost growth up to a plateau.

Temperature

Enzymes like RuBisCO work best around 25–35°C. Extreme heat denatures proteins and slows the Calvin cycle.

Water

Water stress closes stomata, reducing CO₂ intake. Dehydration also disrupts the light reactions.
